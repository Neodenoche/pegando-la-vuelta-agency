The brief

Architecture and nature
in perfect balance.

El Escondite is a collection of private villas built on the ruins of a 130-year-old coffee farm in the mountains of Cundinamarca. Each villa has its own pool, no shared spaces, and a visual identity rooted in Colombian bamboo, clay, and natural fibers.

The challenge was building a site that felt as intimate, calm, and architecturally considered as the property itself — without resorting to generic hospitality templates.

WordPress Custom design Photography direction Booking integration WhatsApp CTA Bilingual
The approach

No stock photography.
No borrowed aesthetics.

The entire visual language was built around the property's own photography — full-bleed hero images, split layouts that let the architecture breathe, and typography with enough weight to hold against dense tropical backgrounds.

The color palette came directly from the property: warm creams, deep earth tones, and the green of the surrounding jungle. Every section was designed to feel like a room, not a page.
